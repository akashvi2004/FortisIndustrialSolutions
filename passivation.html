Passivation Methods

Citric

  • Sanitary-friendly option for many food & hygienic systems
  • Effective removal of free iron / light contamination
  • Lower odor profile and easier handling on-site

Nitric

  • Proven method for demanding industrial service conditions
  • Strong passivation support after fabrication or weld repair
  • Selected when specs require traditional nitric chemistry

Phosphoric

  • Targeted treatment for specific process conditions
  • Used when prep and surface conditioning requirements apply
  • Chosen based on scope, alloy, and acceptance criteria

Process Steps

  1. Assess
  2. Clean / Degrease
  3. Passivate
  4. Rinse / Neutralize
  5. Dry / Inspect
  6. Report (as requested)
